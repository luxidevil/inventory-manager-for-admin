1. What is this app?

Bulk Inventory Manager is a platform for people who buy digital accounts in bulk and resell them with time-limited durations. It keeps track of:

  • What you own — every account email, which batch it came from, and when it expires.
  • What you sold — to whom, for how much, and for how long.
  • Problems — buyers can report dead accounts; the app calculates fair pro-rata refunds and manages replacements.
Think of it as a stock system + sales ledger + after-sales support desk, built specifically for account reselling.

2. Getting started

Register

  1. Open the app and click Register.
  2. Enter your name, email, and a password.
  3. You're in — every new account starts as a Reseller. An admin can upgrade your role later.
💡 If someone sold accounts to you before you registered (as an "external contact"), register with the same email address — your past purchases will automatically link to your new account.

Login

Use your email + password. Your session lasts 7 days, then you'll be asked to log in again. Use the red logout button at the bottom of the sidebar to sign out.

5. Batches & uploading emails

A batch is a group of accounts you acquired together (e.g. "July stock — 30 day accounts"). All inventory enters the system through batches.

Create a batch

  1. Go to Batches → New Batch.
  2. Give it a name (and notes if you like), then save.

Upload emails into a batch

  1. Open the batch and click Upload Emails.
  2. Paste anything — a clean list, a messy chat export, a spreadsheet column. The app automatically extracts every valid email address from the text.
  3. Set the Duration (days) — how long these accounts are valid — and the Start Date. The expiry date of every account is calculated automatically (start + duration).
  4. Review the preview: it shows valid emails, invalid entries, duplicates within your paste, and emails that already exist in inventory.
  5. Confirm. You'll see how many were added and how many were skipped.
🔒 Duplicate protection is absolute. The same email can never exist twice in inventory — even if it was sold before, even if someone else holds it now. Duplicates are always skipped automatically.

You can also edit a batch's name/notes or delete a batch you no longer need.

6. Inventory

The Inventory page lists every individual account record with its email, batch, status, start/expiry dates, and days left.

StatusMeaning
availableIn stock, can be sold.
soldSold to a buyer (the buyer now holds it).
reportedA buyer reported a problem with this account.
  • Filter & search to find specific records or view only expiring ones.
  • Edit a record if you need to correct its details.
  • Remove a record to take it out of inventory (e.g. a dead account you're discarding).

7. Sales

Create a sale

  1. Go to Sales → New Sale.
  2. Choose the Buyer Type:
    • Platform user — someone with an account on this app. They receive the records directly and get a notification.
    • External contact — someone not on the platform yet. Pick an existing contact or add one on the spot (name, email, phone). You can send them an invite link so they can join and claim their purchases later.
  3. Select the inventory records to sell (only available ones are shown).
  4. Set the Price ($), Duration (days), and Start Date, plus optional notes.
  5. Confirm — the records move from "available" to "sold", your batch counters update, and the buyer (if a platform user) is notified instantly.

Renew a sale

When a sale is about to expire, open it and choose Renew: set a new duration and price. The expiry date and each item's days-left are recalculated, and the sale becomes active again. Perfect for subscription-style repeat customers.

My Purchases

If someone sold accounts to you on the platform, they appear in your purchases view — you see exactly which emails you bought, from whom, and when they expire.

8. Contacts & invite links

Contacts are your external customers — buyers who don't have a platform account (yet).

  • Add a contact with name, email, and phone.
  • Generate an invite link and copy it with one click. Send it to your customer via WhatsApp/Telegram/etc.
  • When they open the link and register, their account is automatically linked to all past sales you made to that contact — they instantly see everything they bought from you.
💡 This means you can sell to anyone immediately and let them "claim" their purchase history whenever they're ready to join.

9. Reports — refunds & replacements

When accounts stop working, buyers don't need to argue in chat — they file a Report.

Filing a report (as a buyer)

  1. Open the purchase, click File a Report.
  2. Select the specific items (emails) that have problems and add notes.
  3. Submit. The app automatically calculates a fair refund for each item, pro-rated by remaining time: (price ÷ items ÷ duration) × days left.

Handling a report (as the seller)

  1. You get a notification: "N item(s) from sale have been reported". Open Reports.
  2. For each reported item, choose an action:
    • Replace — provide a replacement email for that item, or use Bulk Replace: paste a list of replacement emails and the app assigns them one-by-one.
    • Refund — accept the calculated refund amount (tracked in Settlements).
  3. Update the report status as you resolve it. Dashboard counters (Pending Refunds / Pending Replacements) track what's still open.

10. Settlements

The money side of reports. Every refund or compensation you owe (or are owed) from a report is tracked as a settlement with a type, amount, status (pending → completed), and notes — so nothing gets forgotten between you and your buyers.

11. Notifications

The Notifications page collects everything that needs your attention:

  • New sale — you received inventory from a seller.
  • Sale reported — a buyer reported items from one of your sales.

Mark items read one-by-one or use read all.

12. Users (admin only)

Visible only to the administrator:

  • See all users with role and active status; search by name or email.
  • Change roles between Bulk Seller / Reseller / Small Seller. The admin role can never be assigned — there is only ever one admin.
  • Activate / deactivate accounts, or delete users.
  • The admin account itself cannot be edited, deactivated, or deleted by anyone.

13. Full walkthrough — your first sale in 5 minutes

  1. Register → log in. You land on the Dashboard (empty for now).
  2. Go to Batches → New Batch → name it My first stock.
  3. Click Upload Emails → paste your account emails → set duration 30 days, start date today → confirm. Your inventory now shows the accounts as available with expiry dates 30 days out.
  4. Go to Contacts → add your customer (name + email).
  5. Go to Sales → New Sale → buyer type External Contact → pick your customer → select the accounts → set price and duration → confirm. Done: items are sold, revenue appears on your dashboard.
  6. Click Copy invite link on the sale/contact and send it to your customer so they can join and see their purchase.
  7. Later: if the customer reports a dead account, you'll get a notification → open Reports → paste a replacement email or approve the auto-calculated refund. Track the money in Settlements.
  8. When the 30 days are nearly up, open the sale → Renew with a new price/duration.

14. Golden rules & FAQ

🔒 No duplicate emails, ever. Inventory can never contain the same email twice — uploads that repeat an existing email are skipped automatically, even if it was sold long ago.

👑 One admin only. The admin role cannot be given, taken, or transferred.

📅 Expiry is automatic. Start date + duration = expiry. Watch the "Expiring Soon" card daily.

FAQ

QuestionAnswer
I pasted 100 emails but fewer were added — why?Some were invalid, repeated within your paste, or already exist in inventory. The upload summary shows exactly how many were skipped and why.
Can I sell to someone who isn't on the platform?Yes — sell to an External Contact, then send them an invite link. Their purchases auto-link when they register with the same email.
How is a refund amount decided?Automatically, pro-rata: the item's share of the sale price × the fraction of days remaining. Nobody has to negotiate.
What happens when a sale expires?The sale shows as expired; use Renew to extend it with a new duration and price.
I forgot my password.Contact the administrator.
